Bio

Alaine is a designer and researcher based in London. Alaine was born in rural Ireland, in border country, but otherwise a part of the world unknown to most, on a smallholding. She spent her formative years drawing, wandering in countryside and building contrapions in my fathers workshop. She learned about ecology and engineering from her father (a farmer and inventor), and empathy and cultivtion from her mother (horticulturist). She has made her career working at the intersection of humans and design, for local government and not-for-profits, helping to shape systems for data sharing and communication around needs and users. Through the early part of her career, working as a designer maker studios in London, she discovered an affinity for human centered design, and began developing tools for collecting meaningfull insights into human needs, and creating actionable steps to there intagration. Alaine is member of a Data Colective, and the founder of OrangegnarO, a small Design Research Consultancy. Both roles are focused on creating open and replicable data structures which empower the user to take control. This work exists in both real excuted projects and in speculative research which aims to inform policy and decision making. In her spare time you will find Alaine between her garden and kitchen, growing, preserving, brewing or baking.
